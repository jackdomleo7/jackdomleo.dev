Level-Up Your Career Today: Developer Edition

$1+

Buy nowBuy on Amazon
Come join us on Product Hunt

Have you wanted to level-up in your career or get a new job in tech? Are you feeling lost on where you need to begin improving? This book may be for you!

This book will explore lots of tips, tricks and techniques for you to kickstart your tech career, get that promotion, create your community online and offline, and level-up your wellbeing and productivity techniques. We also look at self-development in the context of what you can do for yourself to boost and build your portfolio.

This is not a technical book, but one where I chat with you about my own experiences and share with you what has helped me get to where I am today, as someone who has no degree, has been in a tech career for just over 2 years and is super proud of what they have achieved.

If this sounds good to start, then let's start levelling up today!

Why did I write this eBook?

I've been rather successful in my career because I've been smart about the decisions I make, I'm proactive and I know exactly what I need to do to improve my portfolio.

I want to help you help yourself! This eBook is designed to be a light but insightful read and to help make you aware of what you can do for yourself - the book can’t do it for you, only you can level-up your career using this book as a helpful guide. It will help you identify where changes may need to be made in your current situation and what you could do differently.

Who is this eBook for?

This ebook is primarily for, but not limited to, beginners in tech with less than 2 years of professional experience.

Levels (chapters)

  • Introduction
  • Preface
  • Level 0: The Fundamentals of a Career
  • Level 1: Identify Your Path
  • Level 2: Attitude & Professionalism
  • Level 3: Productivity & Mindset
  • Level 4: Portfolio
  • Level 5: Online Presence
  • Level 6: The Job
  • Postface

You can expect to see lots of really useful techniques to help you improve on your career and on your portfolio. There will also be real-life examples from my own portfolio to put certain aspects into context.
